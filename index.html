FAQ

Wolfree is down for me.

Visit the mirrors. If your country has blocked access to the mirrors, enable DNS over HTTPS to override your country's censorship over the internet or download the Tor Browser to access websites your government has banned.

Is Wolfree down? Why can't I access Wolfree?

Your government may have banned access to Wolfree.

  • Enable DoH, e.g., 1.1.1.1.
  • Download a browser that circumvents censorship,
          e.g., Tor Browser.
  • Install a free VPN, e.g., ProtonVPN.

Do I need to use Tor to access Wolfree?

Unless you live in a country without freedom of speech, where reading certain mathematical expressions is banned, then there is no reason to use Tor. But if you can't access Wolfree because it is blocked/banned in your country, then Tor will unlock access.

Will I get in trouble with my internet provider (ISP) or copyright lawyers if I read mathematical expressions from Wolfree?

No case exists of someone being sued for consuming a copyrighted book, song, or movie from a website. Reading mathematical expressions from Wolfree's website isn't filesharing (P2P), so copyright holders can't find out who is visiting Wolfree, and they wouldn't care if they knew.

The programmers of Wolfree are the real targets of the (unsuccessful) lawsuits because they distribute the step-by-step solution, not the users. Thousands of students and scientists around the world safely visit Wolfree every day, so you're in good company.

Why does Wolfree prefer federated social networks?

Further information: http://joinfediverse.wiki
Main article: http://docs.joinmastodon.org
See also: http://torresjrjr.com/archive/2020-07-19-guide-to-the-fediverse

Everybody knows you don't need to use the same email provider to communicate via email. You can send a mail from riseup.net to gmail.com and the other way around. That's about the same way that "federation" in the Fediverse works. Federation just means that different or decentralized servers or instances can communicate with each other via a protocol. Just like email uses POP, IMAP, or SMTP protocols for federation, the Fediverse mostly uses ActivityPub.

Why does Wolfree prefer copyleft licenses?

Further information: http://blueoakcouncil.org/primer
Main article: http://gnu.org/philosophy/free-software-even-more-important.en.html
See also: http://lukesmith.xyz/articles/why-i-use-the-gpl-and-not-cuck-licenses

Copyleft licenses work like permissive licenses with a catch: they require sharing and licensing the source code for new software built with copyleft-licensed software as open software, too. For this reason, copyleft licenses are sometimes called “share-alike” licenses, though “copyleft” is the term you’ll hear most in software.
